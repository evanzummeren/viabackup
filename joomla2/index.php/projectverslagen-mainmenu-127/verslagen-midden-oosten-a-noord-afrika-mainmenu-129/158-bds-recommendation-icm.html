BDS Recommendation ICM

Recommendation to join the Boycott, Divestment and Sanctions campaign (BDS campaign)

Proposed by SCI Catalunya

We recommend to all the branches and recognized groups of SCI  to actively promote and take part in the Boycott,  Divestment and Sanctions (BDS) campaign of the Palestinian civil society in order to react in a non violent way against the apartheid and  occupation policy of the State of Israel. We consider this civic campaign as a means to change situations of injustice and institutional violence and to move towards a more just and peaceful situation.


Background of the recommendation:

During the last meeting of the North South Platform Meeting, held in Barcelona, the MIDI WG  decided to recommend to the ICM that every branch and partner of the movement applies the BDS strategy. Some of the branches of the MIDI WG are already applying this strategy or are implementing some of the actions of the BDS  campaign  (VIA- Netherlands , SCI-Italia, SCI-Belgium, SCI-France, SCI-Catalunya).


What is the BDS campaign?

The BDS campaign is a peace initiative led by civil society organizations, political parties and unions representing the Palestinian people, and is supported by a worldwide network of entities that make up the movement of solidarity with the Palestinian people.  (www.bdsmovement.net)

BDS demand the world to boycott Israel, its institutions and enterprises, and also to withdraw its investments in companies that benefit from the occupation or denial of the rights of the refugee population.    On the site of the bds movement you can find a more detailed explanation about the campaign and country profiles and company information that can help you to further investigate the relations between your country, companies active in your country and their involvement in activities related to Israel’s’ apartheid and occupation policy.


How did the BDS campaign start?

The broad consensus among Palestinian civil society about the need for a broad and sustained Campaign for Boycott, Divestment and Sanctions (BDS) resulted in the Palestinian Call for boycott, divestment and sanctions against Israel that was launched in July 2005 with the initial endorsement of over 170 Palestinian organizations. The signatories to this call represent the three major components of the Palestinian people: the refugees in exile, Palestinians under occupation in the West Bank and Gaza Strip and the subjugated Palestinian citizens of the Israeli state. Meanwhile also some Israeli peace organisations and/or peace activists support this call.

The efforts to coordinate the BDS campaign, that began to grow rapidly since the 2005 call was made public, culminated in the first Palestinian BDS Conference held in Ramallah in November 2007. Out of this conference emerged the BDS National Committee (BNC) as a coordinating body for the BDS campaign within Palestine.

The tasks of the BNC include:

• Campaigning & Awareness Raising, both within Palestine, and by preparing and disseminating resources to campaigns worldwide;
• Holding Conferences & Training local activists and organizations on BDS campaign work;
• International Advocacy, aimed at developing a clear official Palestinian position adopting the BDS campaign, as well as monitoring and confronting international efforts towards normalization with the apartheid regime;
• Rapid Response to projects and initiatives that have normalization with the apartheid regime as their declared or implicit goal, as well as setting clear standards through which initiatives and projects can be assessed;
• Coordinating the BDS Campaign in Arab countries;
• Support & Encouragement for BDS initiatives worldwide;
• Activating & Operating the BDS campaign website (www.bdsmovement.net);
• Media Advocacy and Campaign Materials aimed at disseminating and raising awareness about the importance and development of the BDS movement in Palestine and worldwide;
• Developing and pushing forward the BDS campaign goals, strategies and tactics in accordance with the 2005 Palestinian Civil Society call for BDS.

Why is it important? Why it is good method?

Boycott is one of the most important tools for civilians to act in a nonviolent way towards social change. It is a way to show disagreement with certain policies or with a determined way of acting. The BDS campaign is an important campaign because it is a strategy put forward by the Palestinian civil society in order to react against the occupation and the institutions, and companies collaborating with them, responsible for this occupation. It is a nonviolent method that can be implemented in different degrees. Everybody can choose to which extend he wants to take part it. It is also a long-term campaign to be made step by step. It is also important because it is a united and international campaign where Palestinians, Israelis and international organizations work starting from the same strategy but within their own context. It is possible to share experiences,  make the same actions in different places of the world (e.g. Ahava  boycott day) and work together to realize a certain step. Boycott is the most important tool that has a citizen to act or to protest against an unfair policy.

How can it be introduced within SCI and  why is it a methodology that fits in SCI?

Almost all the existing Palestinian coalitions in Europe and aboard, are implementing the BDS as strategy against the occupation. The  SCI branches involved with the MIDI WG already heard about it and are aware about the actions of boycott that are happening in their countries. If not, it is quite simple to contact the Palestinian solidarity movements of your country and they can provide more information about actions and activities.

It is a methodology that fits in SCI because :

• Our main objective is to fight situations of injustice in a non violent way. This is exactly what the campaign is about.
• SCI is a volunteer and activist organisation that can mobilise volunteer to join this kind of campaigns. Many of the actions (e.g. individual boycott actions or divestment actions) can be done by individual volunteers.
• SCI is a worldwide organisation with a the moment several branches working in Palestine. Giving support to the campaign is also supporting the work of these branches.
• 2 important elements from the BDS campaign are awareness raising and advocacy : locally and internationally. SCI has always used awareness raising campaigns and advocacy campaigns in order to fight injustice.

Motivation

Applying the BDS strategy within SCI will give us more coherence in our policies for choosing partners and contacts, and also in the general political position in this conflict. Even if SCI wants to remain neutral and wants to work for Peace in this geographic area, it is important to tell that BDS is a nonviolent strategy that allows us to define common criteria for working in the area.

The Proposal

We believe that among SCI branches and as an international movement we should try to have common criteria’s and policy in order to be more coherent in the work we do and to be more unified as a social movement and force. This also includes using the same strategy to react against a social reality which we believe is unjust.  The BDS strategy is a global unified strategy where every volunteer and member of the movement can find its own space of action. We know that a global nonviolent strategy will only have results if it is supported by a large number of organisations. We therefore strongly recommend to all SCI branches and SCI International as the co-ordinating body to support this strategy.
