Tanja op Wieringen

Tanja at Wieringen

That’s me in the picture, presenting the sweat lodge we built that we were just about to inaugurate. Native Americans have sweat lodge ceremonies for different kinds of spiritual healings. I can assure you this was a very special experience. It takes quite an effort in mental preparation to get a group of international volunteers (semi) naked in a spiritual sauna. But almost everybody joined the first of three healing rounds in the hot lodge we built together.

The ceremony was a great closure of a wonderful period at the Michaëlshof in Wieringen. This is an ecological spiritual centre, where volunteers can learn about ecologic gardening by working in the permaculture garden. And where groups of people gather for spiritual events. Besides building the lodge out of willow branches, we also built the shelter next to the lodge. First we had to cut some trees to gather the building materials. So we cleaned up a big part of the forest and built a fence of piled branches.  Next to the building activities the volunteers worked in the permaculture vegetable garden every day. From this garden we got our tasty ingredients for the very nice dinners.

Working together and sleeping in one tent together during two weeks of bad weather also caused some individual issues. I was happy to have a  local partner and some other volunteers to consult with, so we could solve the problems together.

I was very positive about my first experience as a camp guide. If I have to possibility I would guide a camp again, and I can recommend everybody who wants to challenge his or her guiding skills to try it next summer!


Tanja Sikkens

Tanja is one of the new faces in VIA. She works as an engineer in Groningen. After attending some nice projects in Slovenia (she is actually half Slovenian) and Itally, she decided she would like to coordinate a project herself, here in Netherlands.
