December 10, 2009: Issue 1

Dear VIA friends and family,

We are excited to send you our first VIA newsletter! This newborn publication is a monthly collection of short news pieces meant to brief all interested VIA associates — young to old, Dutch to Palestinian, work camp alumni to the man on the train who read the back of my bright yellow VIA benefit t-shirt — on the latest happenings within VIA. The newsletter will note the latest workcamps, organizational changes, fundraising events, adventures of members, and any other newsworthy moments that highlight the projects and ideas VIA is focusing on that month.

V-News is published in English, in solidarity with the international component of VIA, and in an effort to reach all interested, involved volunteers, many of whom are not Dutch but are currently living in the Netherlands. For more variety and depth in VIA news and entertainment, be sure to get ahold of the latest VIAdeVIA, our Dutch magazine. Enjoy!

Strategic Steps into 2010

VIA lives on spontaneity — on people with creative ideas and the passion and energy that brings them to life. But in order to maintain continuity and provide a base for coherent, meaningful growth within VIA — as polar to spontaneity as it may sound — our organisation is dependent on a plan.

Following suit from Service Civil International's 2006 flurry of strategic planning, VIA (the Dutch branch of SCI), developed an initial plan during Activist Weekend in fall 2007. After extensive discussions and brainstorm sessions on mission, goals and objectives, detailed action plans were devised during the Annual Meeting in early 2008. That summer, the documents finally shaped into a simple, practical four-year plan, for 2008-2012, detailing specific actions to be taken annually: VIA's first Strategic Plan.

As 2010 creeps in, VIA is recommitting itself to goals in the Strategic Plan. In recent evaluation meetings, core members determined a list of goals for the new year that aim to help VIA grow stronger and more visible as a peace organization, and that at their core reinforce the mission of VIA.*

In 2010, VIA will focus on the following:

o New attention to the PR branch, working to recruit new volunteers to help build the organisation, and move it forward in its mission.

o The importance of diversity and quality within projects. In 2010, we aim to expand our projects so that they bring together different groups within society, not only people from various backgrounds within the work camp. We are also working to offer projects that attract a more diverse group of people — for instance, "older" people — and to offer work weekends instead of work camps, to accommodate busy work schedules.

o Continuing work in conflict areas. However, based on volunteer interest, we have decided to focus for the time being primarily on the Middle East, Central Asia and Northern Africa, through our active MOCANA group.

o Peace education and education on armed conflicts in the world are issues that will take a more prominent place in the study portions of VIA's programs.

The VIA Strategic Plan expires in 2012. By then, we hope to have achieved a majority of our objectives, with energy left to make new plans and continue our work. Our mission is ambitious, but with each small step we take, we hope to contribute to a sustainable, tolerant, sympathetic and non-violent society.

-Floor Bakker

*VIA is a non-profit peace organization dedicated to international volunteer work that promotes cultural exchange and aims to inspire. VIA values sustainability, tolerance, solidarity and non-violence, and stands against a culture of self-interest, profiteering and violence.

VIA Boycotts Too

In 2005, 170 Palestinian civil organisations reacted to violations of human rights of Palestinian people, committed by the Israeli government, by initiating the Boycott, Divestment and Sanctions campaign. The goal of the BDS campaign is to pressure the Israeli government to comply with international human rights law, no matter how many diplomatic or economical ties are put at stake. On top of that, the campaign aims to share information with the international community about current situations in which Palestinians — in the occupied territories and in Israel itself — are finding themselves.

In VIA, we believe that humane governance and active citizenship are key to achieving a more peaceful world. As BDS stands for both, we decided as an organisation to support the campaign. This means that VIA members will help in any way we can to inform Dutch consumers and importing companies of current Israeli-Palestinian politics, and to tell the real story behind some of the products made in Israel, such as illegally-made cosmetics from the Dead Sea.
-Rob van Loenen
More information on VIA and BDS, and how you can contribute, is available at www.stichtingvia.nl

The Fe(sti.via)l

By Florian van Kuijk

On Saturday November 7, VIA-Netherlands organised its first FE(sti.VIA)L for funds and publicity.

There was room for crafts the whole festival long. Little peace flags piled up on one table, while at the other someone was writing a poem. Jelmer and Florian focused the creativity into a peace slogan workshop. Results were painted on a banner, and hung behind the artists. The campfire was lit.
“Words and deeds” is SCI’s creed. So action is never far away. Literally, with the ‘juggling and text’ workshop. All for peace and peace for all. And after a delicious meal we did feel quite peaceful.
But the bar kept us attentive. Drinks were served with an article of the Universal Declaration of Human Rights. Important not to forget. Linda en Bernadette reminded us again with their BDS presentation. Deeds are indeed still needed.
But words came rushing in again with the poets and singer/songwriters. They entertained, confused, raised eyebrows. Michiel Lieuwma’s songs inspired laughter. Sieger tried to eliminate his electronic counterpart by trashing a speaker box.
Luckily no harm was done, and the musicians plugged themselves in. Joris Sikken’s played his first gig with a new band. You wouldn’t have said. De Halve Perziken op Zware Siroop almost blew the roof off. This naturally resulted in dancing. And the night was skilfully concluded with Rigas’ mysterious friends. Their jazz created an atmosphere in perfect balance with the last flames of campfire. Outside it had stopped raining.

Many thanks to everyone who attended and to EMMA for their hospitality.
