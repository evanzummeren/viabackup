A Palestinian Supper

During a meeting in Barcelona the idea of a dinner party was introduced: ask your volunteers returning from projects all over the world to cook up a dinner with food from the countries they visted and organize short presentations about their experiences.

The people who are now active in the Middle-East group of VIA decided to kick-off: On Wednesday, the 16th of December, they organised the Palestinian Dinner Party.

Of course there was Palestinian food, but that was not all! The hall between the office and the kitchen is usually quite a boring place, but we managed to transform it into a cozy ‘christmas-market’ where several stands were located. Palestinian music, Christmas-lights and the unpleasant TL-light dimmed by several Palestinian flags created the atmosphere. People could buy Palestinian olive oil, olives, almonds and lots of other food, as well as postcards and several types of handmade crafts. All to stimulate the Palestinian economy and to make it possible for Palestinians to build up an independent life.

Naturally, we also presented information about VIA and SCI, and the last SCI-agendas of 2010 were sold.

Thanks to the cook Marisca and a lot of helpers, there was a huge dinner, consisting of Mujadderah as main dish, together with many salads and dips, like mtabbel, hoummous, tabouleh en salata bakdoensiyye. Even the volunteers worked hours on wrapping wineleaves around a filling… The Palestinian products from Canaan, which are fairtrade and mainly organic, were used as much as possible. We demonstrated that Palestinian food can also be prepared vegetarian.

Elegant menus told the story about olives. No Palestinian meal (breakfast, lunch or dinner alike) lacks olive oil and olives. The olive tree has deep roots and is the symbol for the connection between the Palestinians and their ground, as well as a symbol of fertility and hope.

The dinner was split into several courses by short presentations given by the volunteers, some about their projects in Nablus and Bethlehem (Palestine), but also in a Palestinian refugee camp in Syria. The volunteers also explained what they have been doing since they returned to The Netherlands. There was a real-live show by the bathrobe brigade (see: mooiniet.wordpress.com), and Rob described the BDS (Boycot, Divestment and Sanctions) campaign in the Netherlands and the role of VIA (see www.BDSmovement.org).

 

It was really great to host around 35 people on a Wednesday evening, and inspired lots of energy. A nice mix of people attended, from (old) VIA activists, to friends and family of the volunteers. The movie was cancelled, because people preferred to talk to one another. And that is what a dinner party is all about: meeting people, talking about your experiences, ideas, plans… I really hope there will be a next dinner party soon, where I can learn about another part of the world!
