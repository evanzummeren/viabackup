Column: Florian's Traveling Plea

 

Plane, train or bus or just plain walking?

Now, it’s almost time to go out into the world, or perhaps you are already on your way.

When going to Belgium or Germany it’s easy enough to take a train or a bus, but when you are going to a middle-European country it’s tempting to catch a plane. It’s fast and there are so many cheap airlines around. They’re like internet community sites, you know they’re kind of bad for you, but you can’t leave it and everybody’s doing it. But let me reaffirm it now: Planes are bad, yes evil even...

I won’t deny it’s quite an achievement to make so many tons of metal lift off. Just amazing that all the machinery fit together so perfectly that it just does that. Try jumping up yourself and you will just return to earth again. So applause is in order. There have been some engineers working on these constructions we call planes and they know their jobs. And then you have the pilots manoeuvring them through our crowded airspace and onto and off runways. It’s like playing Mario but not be allowed to loose and actually making it. Applause? A standing ovation is more appropriate.

And occasionally it goes terribly wrong, which is the frightening bit. A bolt breaks off or a computer goes mad or the pilot was just out for a smoke, and the whole goes tumbling down like a kite. This could be a reason to consider another mode of transport.
But don’t.
Well, do.
But on your own account. I understand it’s no fun travelling while thinking you are going to die. But let me tell you that you are not, not yet in any case. Just count the number of flights and count the number that crash. It should comfort you. Numberwise, statistically if you like, planes aren’t bound to crash. You are much more likely to be hit by a car on any average street. This happens more often. It’s a sad affair. We wouldn’t wish it to anyone. My point is that you are never totally safe, when travelling, when living.

So let’s think happy thoughts.

And let me explain to you why not to fly, if not the fear. A bigger more real reason could be that taxes on kerosene are kept low, partly on purpose, partly due to complications in international laws (like, in which country to pay them). In this way airlines can keep ticketprises low, while venting enormous amounts of CO2.

This is something VIA does not approve of. And I don’t approve either to be honest. In fact CO2 and his friends the Greenhouse gasses always come to your party uninvited, get ridiculously drunk and then start to tell embarrassing jokes and insult people. They can really ruin the atmosphere. This would be a reason to look for other options. And this is your lucky day, because they exist. And if you are a traveller and you are because you are reading this, then you have the chance to put other modes of transport on the map again.


First we build our very own vulcano! But admittedly that can also ruin the atmosphere, as volcanos a socially inapt and they do it in the corner of your room and you have to clean the whole mess up.

So consider this: Specially on short distances there is really no reason to take a plane, when cheap alternatives are available. Arguably not cheaper, but really.

Take just a bit more effort in planning an taking the trip to your project and it can be just as big an experience as the project itself, in the good way, and else trust me it will turn into a adventurous memory. Plus you get a sense of actually travelling, seeing the landscape go by, reading a book, getting totally bored... Okay, but that also happens on a plane now, doesn’t it? Being bored is more or less part of any holiday. It’s when you start to unwind, relax, take it easy.

Mind you, don’t turn into an eco-freak. As VIA we want you being a peace-freak and most people can be only one freak at the time. But still, it’s easy enough to travel with a smaller footprint.

And if you really want to have no footprint at all, you can just stay home... No don’t do that. Go save the world!

You know what I’m doing after taking flights previous years? I’m taking my bicycle this summer, and am going to try to reach my project paddling. Because if you think planes are amazing, marvel to the possibility that you can transport yourself thousands of kilometres by just the power of your own legs.


Florian
